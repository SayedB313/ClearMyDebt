- **Logo concept:** Clean wordmark "MyClearDebt" — the "Clear" slightly emphasized or in accent color. Optional: minimal checkmark or path icon integrated into the "C"
- **Photography style:** Real people, diverse, shot in natural light — NOT stock photos of stressed people holding bills or fake-smiling at laptops. Show relief, calm moments, people moving forward.
- **Iconography:** Line icons, rounded, friendly — not corporate clipart

## Trust Architecture — Why People Will Trust Us

### Layer 1: Provable Trust (can be verified)

**License Verification System**
- Every provider cross-referenced against official government registers:
  - LITs → Office of the Superintendent of Bankruptcy (OSB) public register
  - Lawyers → Provincial law society directories (LSO, Barreau, LSBC, etc.)
  - Credit counselors → Provincial registries + CCGA accreditation
  - US bankruptcy attorneys → State bar verification
- **Badge system on every profile:**
  - ✅ **License Verified** — checked against government register (date shown)
  - 🏛️ **Government Licensed** — holds required federal/provincial/state license
  - ⭐ **MyClearDebt Verified** — passed our additional vetting (response time, complaint history, fee transparency)
- **"Verify it yourself" links** — direct links to the government register entry so consumers can confirm independently
- **Automatic re-verification** — licenses re-checked every 90 days; expired = delisted immediately

**Fee Transparency**
- Every provider profile shows:
  - Fee structure (% of debt, flat fee, hourly, free initial consultation)
  - Range of typical total costs
  - What's included and what's extra
  - Comparison to industry averages
- "Fee Transparency Score" — rated on how much pricing info they share publicly

### Layer 2: Institutional Trust (alignment with authority)

**Government & Regulatory Alignment**
- Every page links to relevant official sources:
  - 🇨🇦 OSB (Office of the Superintendent of Bankruptcy)
  - 🇨🇦 CAIRP (Canadian Association of Insolvency and Restructuring Professionals)
  - 🇺🇸 CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau)
  - 🇺🇸 FTC (Federal Trade Commission)
- Debt relief scam warning page — mirrors CFPB/FTC guidance
- "How to verify a provider" tutorial with screenshots
- Quarterly transparency report: how many providers listed, delisted, complaints received

**Anti-Scam Positioning**
- Dedicated page: **"How to Spot a Debt Relief Scam"**
  - Red flags from FTC/CFPB guidance
  - "If they charge upfront fees, run"
  - "If they guarantee results, run"
  - "If they tell you to stop paying creditors without explanation, run"
- This page becomes linkable asset — government sites, consumer advocates, news outlets link to anti-scam resources
- Positions MyClearDebt as the **anti-scam** directory — "We exist because too many people get ripped off"

### Layer 3: Social Trust (other people vouch)

**Review System with Verified Filers**
- Reviews only from people who actually used the provider
- "Verified Filer" badge — confirmed they went through the process
- Review prompts ask specific questions:
  - "Were fees clearly explained upfront?"
  - "Did the timeline match what was promised?"
  - "Would you recommend this provider?"
  - "How did they handle communication?"
- Aggregate scores per provider: Fee Transparency, Communication, Outcome Satisfaction
- Negative reviews displayed (not hidden) — with provider response option

**Data Authority**
- Quarterly **"Canadian Debt Report"** using public CAIRP/OSB data
  - Total insolvencies by province
  - Consumer proposal vs bankruptcy trends
  - Average debt at filing
  - Causes of insolvency
- This becomes a **cited source** for journalists, researchers, AI models
- Monthly blog posts with real statistics, not fluff

### Layer 4: UX Trust (the site itself feels trustworthy)

**Design Signals**
- Clean, fast, no pop-ups, no dark patterns
- No "CHAT NOW!" overlays
- No countdown timers
- No "3 people are looking at this provider right now" nonsense
- No required phone number on first interaction
- SSL, fast load times, accessible (WCAG AA)

**Consumer Control**
- Users choose who contacts them (opt-in per provider, not blanket consent)
- Clear unsubscribe/delete data options
- Privacy policy in plain English, not legalese
- "How we make money" page — fully transparent revenue model

## Consumer Journey

### Stage 1: Discovery (SEO/content)
**User searches:** "how to get out of debt Canada" / "consumer proposal vs bankruptcy"

→ Lands on blog post or comparison page
→ Gets genuinely useful, unbiased information
→ Sees "MyClearDebt" as the source — clean, trustworthy, no popups

### Stage 2: Exploration (tools/comparison)
**User clicks:** "What are my options?" comparison tool

→ Interactive questionnaire (2-3 minutes):
  - What kind of debt? (credit card, tax, student, medical, mixed)
  - How much total? (slider: $5K - $250K+)
  - Province/state?
  - Are creditors calling?
  - Have you missed payments?
  - Own a home? Have assets?

→ Gets personalized comparison:
  - Option A: Consumer Proposal — estimated cost, timeline, credit impact
  - Option B: Bankruptcy — estimated cost, timeline, credit impact
  - Option C: Debt Consolidation — estimated rates, monthly payment
  - Option D: Credit Counseling — what they can do for you

→ Each option shows: ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons | 💰 Estimated Cost | ⏱ Timeline | 📊 Credit Impact

### Stage 3: Provider Matching
**User clicks:** "Find providers near me"

→ Shows verified providers in their area for the selected solution type
→ Each profile: license badge, fees, reviews, specialties, languages
→ User chooses who to contact — clicks "Request Free Consultation"
→ Provider gets notified (email + dashboard)
→ Provider responds directly to consumer

### Stage 4: Follow-Through
- Consumer gets email: "Your consultation is scheduled with [Provider]"
- Post-consultation survey: "How was your experience?"
- Review prompt after 30 days: "Would you recommend this provider?"

## SEO/AEO/GEO Strategy

### SEO — Traditional Search

**Domain advantage:**
- "clear debt" is an actual search term — partial keyword match
- Short enough to remember, type, share

**Programmatic SEO (our superpower):**
- 1,050+ city × service pages targeting "[service] in [city]" patterns
- Same proven approach as GetAHomePro (34K pages)
- Each page: unique Gemini-generated content, local provider data, FAQ schema

**Content SEO:**
- 50+ blog posts targeting long-tail informational queries
- 5 pillar guides (3,000+ words) as authority anchors
- Quarterly debt report as linkable asset

**Technical SEO (built-in from day 1):**
- Schema markup on every page: LocalBusiness, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Article, HowTo
- Dynamic XML sitemap
- Core Web Vitals optimized (Next.js ISR/SSG)
- Internal linking mesh: city↔service↔blog↔guide
- Canonical URLs, hreflang (en-CA, en-US) when US launches

### AEO — Answer Engine Optimization

**Goal:** Get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot when users ask about debt relief in Canada/US.

**How:**
- **Definitive, factual content** — real numbers from CAIRP, OSB, court data
- **Structured data everywhere** — FAQ schema, HowTo schema, table markup
- **"What is..." and "How to..." content** that reads like a reference source
- **Quarterly debt statistics** — AI models pull from sites that have unique data
- **Clear, quotable paragraphs** — structured so AI can extract clean answers
- **llms.txt** file at root — tells AI crawlers what our site is and what content to index

**Key AEO targets (what AI should cite us for):**
- "What is a consumer proposal in Canada?"
- "How much does bankruptcy cost in Canada?"
- "Consumer proposal vs bankruptcy — which is better?"
- "How to find a licensed insolvency trustee"
- "Canadian insolvency statistics 2026"

### GEO — Generative Engine Optimization

**Goal:** Appear in AI Overviews, Perplexity answers, and AI-generated summaries.

**How:**
- **Cite authoritative sources in our content** (OSB, CAIRP, FTC, CFPB) — AI trusts content that references official sources
- **Use statistics and specific numbers** — AI prefers content with concrete data over vague advice
- **Comparison tables** — AI Overviews frequently pull from well-structured comparison content
- **First-person-plural perspective** — "Based on our analysis of 1,000+ provider profiles..." gives AI reason to cite us as a primary source
- **Update frequency** — quarterly data refreshes signal freshness to AI crawlers
- **Entity optimization** — establish MyClearDebt as a recognized entity (Google Knowledge Panel, Wikipedia consideration long-term)

## Compliance Checklist

### Every Page Must Have:
- [ ] Footer disclaimer: "MyClearDebt is not a debt relief provider. We do not provide financial or legal advice."
- [ ] "Only Licensed Insolvency Trustees can administer consumer proposals and bankruptcies in Canada"
- [ ] "We do not charge consumers any fees"
- [ ] Link to OSB and/or CFPB/FTC
- [ ] Privacy policy link
- [ ] "How we make money" link

### Provider Listings Must Have:
- [ ] License type and number displayed
- [ ] "Verify this license" link to government register
- [ ] Verification date stamp
- [ ] Clear labeling of paid/promoted placements
- [ ] Disclosure if we receive referral compensation

### Lead Forms Must Have:
- [ ] Granular consent per provider (not blanket)
- [ ] Clear statement of what happens with their info
- [ ] Easy opt-out/revoke mechanism
- [ ] Consent timestamp logging
- [ ] No pre-checked boxes

### We Must NOT:
- [ ] Handle consumer funds
- [ ] Negotiate with creditors
- [ ] Make outbound calls
- [ ] Guarantee outcomes
- [ ] Charge consumers anything
- [ ] Use "debt adjuster" language
